Several foreigners who fly to our country have seen children with prominent rib cages and parents tired from the unceasing search for sustenance, they have witnessed the agonizing impacts of food insecurity hidden in our country. Even I, as a Filipino citizen, cannot deny the fact that our country is drowning in the depravity of the lack of food security. According to statistics, approximately half of the country is affected by it.

The IPC (Integrated Food Security Phase Classification) has gathered information regarding the percentage of chronic food insecurity reeking in the Philippines from the years 2015 to 2020. Their statistics show that around 64% of our population is suffering from food insecurity. Specifically, 38% experience mild food insecurity, 17% are moderate, and 8% suffer severely. Researchers infer that the food insecurity endured by the Philippine populace is driven by poor food consumption quality and a high level of chronic undernutrition. These statistics corroborate how immense the enormity of food insecurity in our country is.
